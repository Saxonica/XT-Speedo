XT-Speedo reports explained

Performance metric

In the overview table, as our "bottom-line" metric we use the sum of the process times (over all tests) for the driver, relative to the sum for the baseline driver. This of course gives greater weighting to tests which take longer; a different choice of computation could well give a different picture. We also give the minimum and maximum values of the relative times for the individual tests, to give an idea of the spread.

Measured times and averages

The times for the three processes for each test are calculated as the average time over a number of runs. Currently for each process we run two blocks of iterations, calculating the average time from the 2nd block. For each block, we iterate a process at least 5 times, and for at least 1 second.

Relative times

For individual test processes, we look at the performance times relative to the baseline driver. A value of 2 means that the driver takes 2 times as long to perform the process (i.e. it is 2 times slower than the baseline). A value of 0.5 means that the driver takes half the time to perform the process (i.e. it is 2 times faster than the baseline).

Bar charts

The bar charts plot a driver's process speeds for each test relative to the baseline driver. The axis represents the baseline driver speed. Bars above the axis mean that the driver performs a test slower than the baseline, and bars below the axis mean that the driver is faster than the baseline. The height of the bars corresponds to the number of times slower (above the axis) or faster (below the axis) that the driver is compared to the baseline. (This is achieved by using a reciprocal scale below the axis.)

Bars are highlighted and shown with an arrow head to indicate that a test result is off the scale of the chart, which is based on the 5th to 95th percentile range. The actual numbers are available in the detailed results table, or can be obtained by hovering the pointer over the bar.
